# AI for Everyone – Comprehensive Workshop Plan

**Workshop Duration:** 1 Day (6–8 hours)

**Objective:** Equip participants with foundational AI knowledge, hands-on skills with AI tools, and practical frameworks to apply AI safely and effectively in real-world scenarios.

---

## Session Breakdown and Facilitation Notes

### **Session 1: Introduction & Pre-Course Quiz (30 mins)**

* **Activities:** Welcome participants, outline objectives, and conduct icebreaker.
* **Pre-Course Quiz:** Gauge baseline AI knowledge.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Encourage discussion, normalize curiosity, and reduce fear. Use live polls or interactive quizzes for engagement.
* **Logistics:** Prepare slides, printed quiz sheets or digital polling tools.

### **Session 2: Understanding AI (45 mins)**

* **Content:** Explain AI concepts, LLMs, tokenization, AI strengths, and limitations.
* **Knowledge Check:** Short interactive questions or Kahoot-style quiz.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Use simple analogies; demonstrate AI examples live.
* **Logistics:** Ensure a projector/screen setup, stable internet for live AI demos.

### **Session 3: AI Tools Overview (45 mins)**

* **Content:** Introduce Google Gemini and NotebookLM, highlighting use cases for creative vs. grounded tasks.
* **Hands-On Demo:** Quick live demo with sample tasks.
* **Knowledge Check:** Ask participants to identify which tool suits which task.
* **Logistics:** Ensure participant accounts/access for tools, reliable Wi-Fi, and laptops ready.

### **Break (15 mins)**

* Ensure refreshments are available; encourage informal networking.

### **Session 4: Prompting & P.R.T.C. Framework (1 hr)**

* **Content:** Explain structured prompting using P.R.T.C. (Persona, Request, Target, Constraints).
* **Activity:** Participants practice crafting prompts.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Walk around to provide guidance, highlight common mistakes.
* **Logistics:** Provide worksheet templates and example prompts.

### **Session 5: Hands-On Lab 1 – Creative & Grounded Outputs (1 hr)**

* **Activities:** Participants draft articles, summaries, and analyze documents using AI tools.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Pair participants for collaborative learning; provide examples.
* **Logistics:** Ensure stable Wi-Fi, pre-uploaded sample documents, access to tools, headphones for audio outputs.

### **Lunch Break (1 hr)**

* Allow time for informal discussions and networking.

### **Session 6: AI in Daily Life (1 hr)**

* **Content:** Demonstrate practical applications: spreadsheets, meal planning, homework assistance.
* **Activity:** Participants apply AI to real-life mini-projects.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Encourage sharing of ideas; monitor for questions.
* **Logistics:** Pre-load example spreadsheets and datasets.

### **Session 7: Ethics, Hallucinations, and Verification (45 mins)**

* **Content:** Discuss AI hallucinations, verification methods, ethical use.
* **Activity:** Case studies and group discussion.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Emphasize human oversight; encourage debate.
* **Logistics:** Prepare handouts or slides with real-life examples.

### **Session 8: Machine Learning Essentials (30 mins)**

* **Content:** Overview of supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning; overfitting and underfitting.
* **Knowledge Check:** Quick Q&A or interactive quiz.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Use visual diagrams; keep explanations simple.

### **Session 9: Hands-On Lab 2 – Real-World Use Cases (1 hr)**

* **Activities:** Excel automation, project planning, recipe/meal planning; combine AI tools for efficiency.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Circulate, assist participants, encourage experimentation.
* **Logistics:** Provide datasets, sample documents, and templates.

### **Session 10: Post-Course Reflection & Quiz (30 mins)**

* **Activity:** Post-course quiz, reflection discussion, action planning.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Compare pre- and post-course answers; highlight growth.

### **Closing & Key Takeaways (15 mins)**

* **Content:** Summarize core concepts, tools, and frameworks; open Q&A.
* **Facilitation Notes:** Share next steps and resources; encourage continued learning.

---

## Workshop Logistics & Preparation

### **Venue & Equipment:**

* Quiet, well-lit room with tables for group work.
* Projector or large screen; stable Wi-Fi.
* Laptops for all participants (or BYOD policy).
* Headphones for audio labs.
* Power strips and extension cords.
* Flipcharts, markers, and printed handouts.

### **Participant Preparation:**

* Confirm registration and pre-course instructions.
* Bring laptop and charger.
* Optional: Bring documents (PDFs, spreadsheets) for hands-on labs.
* Pre-install access to AI tools if accounts are required.
* Encourage open-mindedness and willingness to experiment.

### **Trainer/Facilitator Preparation:**

* Review workshop slides, labs, and knowledge checks.
* Test all AI tools and internet connectivity.
* Prepare example prompts and documents.
* Plan for participant support during hands-on exercises.
* Prepare strategies to manage time, pacing, and participant questions.

### **Pre-Workshop Checks:**

* Confirm Wi-Fi and network access.
* Ensure all software and accounts are accessible.
* Print handouts and quiz sheets.
* Arrange refreshments and seating.
* Prepare a schedule with clear timings and session objectives.

### **Post-Workshop:**

* Distribute post-course resources, slides, and recordings.
* Collect feedback and post-course quizzes.
* Provide guidance for continued learning and application of AI tools.
